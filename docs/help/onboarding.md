# Onboarding — Help

Draft v1. Edit freely.

## What this page is for

This is where you turn an interested trucker into a **fully onboarded carrier** — by collecting their paperwork and confirming their profile. Use it after a trucker has said "yes, I want to work with you."

The page has two parts: a list of truckers being onboarded on the left, and a profile + document checklist on the right.

## How to use it — common workflows

### Onboard a new carrier from scratch

1. From the **Truckers** page, click the trucker and use "Start Onboarding" (or pick them from the list on this page).
2. Fill in the **Trucker Profile**: city, state, number of trucks, truck kinds (Dry Van, Reefer, etc.), and truck dimensions.
3. Toggle the three switches at the top if they apply to this carrier:
   - **Uses Factoring** — adds Factoring Notice of Assignment to the required-docs list
   - **New Authority** — adds documents specific to new authorities
   - **Uses Quick Pay** — adds Quick Pay agreement docs
4. Under **Routes & Availability**, add their **Preferred Lanes**, **Operating States** (places they'll drive), **Avoid States** (places they refuse), and **Preferred Days**.
5. Upload each required document under **Delivery Stages**. Examples: W9, MC Authority, COI, Voided Check.
6. When every required document is uploaded, the **"Mark Fully Onboarded"** button becomes active. Click it to graduate them to the Onboarded Truckers list.

### Replace a document the carrier sent a corrected version of

1. Open the trucker.
2. Find the document under Delivery Stages — it will show "Uploaded" with a Replace link.
3. Click **Replace** and pick the new file. The old one is kept in history.

## What each button does

- **Uses Factoring / New Authority / Uses Quick Pay** — toggles that change which documents are required for this trucker.
- **Upload** (on each document slot) — attach the carrier's PDF/image for that document.
- **Replace** — swap in a corrected version of an already-uploaded document.
- **Mark Fully Onboarded** — only enabled once all required documents are on file. Moves the carrier into the Onboarded Truckers list and out of the active onboarding funnel.

## Why some documents are conditional

A factoring company asks for different paperwork than a quick-pay carrier; a brand-new authority needs documents an established carrier doesn't. The three toggles tell the system which documents matter for this particular carrier, so the checklist isn't padded with paperwork they don't need to submit.
